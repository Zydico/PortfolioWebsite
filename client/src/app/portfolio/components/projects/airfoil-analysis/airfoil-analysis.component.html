Airfoil Analysis Group Project
October 2021 - December 2022
This was a group project taken during the MAE 335 class of Fall 2022 at WVU (Incompressible Aerodynamics). We were a group of 3 with the goal of the project being to learn and use the XFLR5 software to perform analysis on the NACA 23015 and NACA 1408 airfoils. We were provided variables such as Wing Planform Area, Taper Ratio, Aspect Ratio, and Cruise Velocity, and were tasked to find the effects that variables such as Aspect Ratio, Sweep, and Taper Ratio have on the lift, drag, and moment coefficients. But before analysis could be done, a panel independence study was performed to find the minimum number of panels that were necessary to perform the analysis, as to not have to perform unnecessary computations so that the analysis time would be greatly shortened.
